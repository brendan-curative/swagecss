Your Medications

Let's Make Your Medication Routine Easier

We know life gets busy and staying on top of prescriptions can be tough.

Whether it's remembering daily doses, managing side effects, or wondering if your meds are really working, you're not alone.

Here are some simple tips you can take today to stay on track with your medication routine.

1. Build Your Routine

Small daily cues help turn medication time into second nature.

  • Set Reminders — Use a phone app or alarm to keep your schedule on track. (Try Round, Medisafe, MyTherapy, or Mango CARE.)
  • Keep Your Meds in Sight — Store them somewhere visible, like your kitchen counter or bedside table.
  • Link to Daily Habits — Pair your meds with something you already do — brushing your teeth, making coffee, or walking the dog.

2. Stay Organized and Prepared

Reduce stress by keeping everything in order and easy to access.

  • Find the $0 Option — Many conditions have medications available at $0 through the Curative formulary. Ask your provider or pharmacist to check for $0 preferred prescriptions to keep your care affordable and consistent. You can reach out to Curative anytime as well as check curative.com/drugs.
  • Automate Refills — Ask your pharmacy about auto-refills or syncing prescriptions so they're ready at once.
  • Try Home Delivery — Many pharmacies — including Curative Pharmacy — deliver prescriptions for free. Fewer trips, fewer missed doses.
  • Simplify Your Routine — Use a pill organizer to see what you've taken. You can request one for free from Curative Pharmacy.
  • Understand Your Meds — Make a quick list of what each medication treats. It helps you understand why they matter and how they're connected.

3. Get Support When You Need It

You don't have to figure this out alone.

  • Ask Questions Early — If you're unsure about side effects, cost, or how to take a medication, your pharmacist or doctor can help.
  • Reach Out To Us — Your Curative team — from pharmacists to Care Navigators — is here to help you feel confident and supported every step of the way.

Taking medications consistently keeps you healthier, longer. Start with one or two of these small changes — and build from there.
