Your Screenings

Screenings help you stay informed about your health and guide conversations with your provider. These preventive services are usually covered at $0 under your plan.*

Please update your screening status below and indicate if you would like additional information about the screening. If you have a family history that changes your risk, please discuss it with a Curative Clinician in Part 2 or speak with your Primary Care Physician.

Cervical Cancer Screening

Last completed:
What it is: Pap or HPV test done by a PCP or OB/GYN to check for early cell changes.
Recommendation: Women 21-65, every 3-5 years depending on test type and history.

Breast Cancer Screening

Last completed:
What it is: X-ray of the breast to look for early signs of cancer. Additional imaging (ultrasound or MRI) may be recommended for dense breasts or higher risk.
Recommendation: Women 40-74, every 1-2 years.

Colon Cancer Screening

Last completed:
What it is: A colonoscopy (a specialist uses a small camera to check your colon while you're relaxed or asleep) or an at-home stool test like Cologuard. Colonoscopies are performed by a gastroenterologist and are usually ordered by your primary care provider (PCP).
Recommendation: Adults 45-75. Stool tests are typically done every year; colonoscopies about every 10 years, unless your provider recommends earlier or more frequent testing.

Lung Cancer Screening

Last completed:
What it is: Low-dose CT scan to look for early lung changes.
Recommendation: Adults 50-80 with a history of heavy smoking, typically yearly, if eligible.

Prostate Cancer Screening

Last completed:
What it is: Blood test measuring PSA, a protein made by the prostate.
Recommendation: Men 55-69, every 1-2 years, based on shared decision-making with your provider.

Skin Cancer Screening

Last completed:
What it is: Visual skin exam by a PCP or dermatologist to look for new or changing moles or spots.
Recommendation: Adults 20+, as needed based on risk; annual checks may be recommended for higher-risk individuals.
