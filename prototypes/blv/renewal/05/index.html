Your Providers

Reflect and Check

Let's make sure everything in your care profile is current. We'll also highlight a few tips that can help you get even more value from your plan.

Primary Care Provider:

Your Care Navigator will assist you with that

Your PCP is your main point of care. Having a primary care provider means:

  • One person knows your full health story.
  • You can stay on track to get preventive care and screenings on time.
  • It's easier to catch small issues before they become big ones.

Other Providers:

Have specialists, therapists, or other clinicians you see regularly?

Your PCP is your main point of care. Having a primary care provider means:

  1. Go to Provider Search. You'll see doctors, nurse practitioners, facilities and other types of providers covered at $0.
  2. Filter by what matters to you. Choose by location, specialty, gender, or language.
  3. Check availability and schedule. Call to ask about earliest appointment availability.

Need help? Your Care Navigator can walk you through Provider Search.
